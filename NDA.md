---

## 1. Purpose
The Parties wish to exchange information to evaluate a business relationship relating to **FraudLens** (fraud management dashboard, reporting automation, and audit/evidence tooling) (the “**Purpose**”).

---

## 2. Confidential Information
“Confidential Information” means any non-public information disclosed by a Party (“Disclosing Party”) to the other (“Receiving Party”), including:
- product designs, architectures, pricing, roadmaps;
- technical docs, API keys/tokens, credentials, source code;
- security controls, incident data, logs, customer data;
- contracts and business plans;
whether disclosed in writing, orally, visually, or electronically.

Exclusions: information that is (a) public through no fault of the Receiving Party, (b) already known, (c) independently developed, or (d) rightfully received from a third party without duty of confidentiality.

---

## 3. Obligations
Receiving Party will:
- use Confidential Information only for the Purpose;
- protect it with at least reasonable care (and no less than it protects its own confidential info);
- restrict access to employees/agents with a need to know, bound by confidentiality obligations;
- not disclose it to third parties without written consent, except as permitted below.

---

## 4. Permitted disclosures
Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information:
- to professional advisors under confidentiality; and/or
- if required by law/regulator/court, provided it gives prompt notice (if legally permitted) and cooperates to seek protective treatment.

---

## 5. Term and survival
- Term of disclosures: **[12–24] months** from Effective Date.
- Confidentiality obligations survive for **[3–5] years** after termination (or longer for trade secrets to the extent permitted by law).

---

## 6. Return / destruction
On request, Receiving Party will return or destroy Confidential Information, except one archival copy may be retained solely for compliance/legal purposes.

---

## 7. No license
No IP rights are granted except as necessary for the Purpose.

---

## 8. No warranty
Confidential Information is provided “as is”; no warranties are given as to accuracy or completeness.

---

## 9. Remedies
Unauthorized disclosure may cause irreparable harm; the Disclosing Party may seek injunctive relief in addition to other remedies.
