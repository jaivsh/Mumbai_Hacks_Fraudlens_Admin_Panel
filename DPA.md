# FraudLens — Data Processing Addendum (DPA) (Template)

> **Important**: Template only; not legal advice. Banks will often require specific clauses, annexes, and security schedules. Customize before signing.

**Last updated:** 2026-04-03  
This Data Processing Addendum (“**DPA**”) forms part of the agreement between:
- **Customer** (Controller), and
- **FraudLens (Project Team)** (Processor) (“Provider”).

This DPA applies when Provider processes **Personal Data** on behalf of Customer in connection with FraudLens.

---

## 1. Definitions
- **Personal Data**, **Processing**, **Controller**, **Processor**: as defined under applicable law (e.g., India DPDP Act 2023, GDPR where applicable).
- **Security Incident**: accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data.

---

## 2. Processing details
### 2.1 Subject matter
Fraud management dashboards, incident workflows, report generation, evidence/audit services, and assistant chatbot (if enabled).

### 2.2 Duration
For the term of the services, plus retention configured or legally required.

### 2.3 Nature and purpose
Hosting, storage, generation of reports/drafts, incident investigation support, audit logging, and retrieval.

### 2.4 Types of Personal Data (examples)
User identifiers, contact details, transaction identifiers, IP/device/location signals, and report content as provided by Customer.

### 2.5 Categories of Data Subjects (examples)
Customer employees/users, end customers of Customer, and counterparties in transactions.

---

## 3. Processor obligations
Provider will:
- process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer;
- ensure personnel confidentiality;
- implement appropriate security measures;
- assist Customer with data subject requests to the extent applicable;
- notify Customer of Security Incidents without undue delay;
- delete/return Personal Data at end of services (subject to retention needs);
- make available information necessary to demonstrate compliance (e.g., security documentation, audit reports where available).

---

## 4. Customer obligations
Customer will:
- ensure a lawful basis for processing and sharing Personal Data;
- provide lawful instructions and ensure data minimization;
- manage access control and roles for its users;
- handle regulator reporting obligations unless explicitly contracted otherwise.

---

## 5. Subprocessors
Customer authorizes Provider to use subprocessors as needed to provide the Service (e.g., cloud hosting providers). Provider will maintain an up-to-date list and, where feasible, provide notice of material changes.

---

## 6. Security measures (summary)
Provider maintains reasonable safeguards, including (examples):
- access controls and role-based authorization,
- encryption in transit,
- audit logging,
- incident monitoring,
- integrity checks for stored artifacts (hashing).

Detailed controls may be attached as **Schedule 1 (Security Measures)**.

---

## 7. Security incident notification
Provider will:
- notify Customer upon confirming a Security Incident affecting Personal Data;
- provide reasonable information about scope, impact, and remediation;
- cooperate with Customer’s investigation and regulatory obligations.

---

## 8. Data transfers
Processing locations depend on the configured cloud region(s). Customer may require regional residency; add as a contractual requirement if needed.

---

## 9. Audit
Customer may request reasonable information to verify compliance. Any on-site audit is subject to scheduling, confidentiality, and security constraints.

---

## 10. Liability
Liability under this DPA follows the main agreement unless required otherwise by applicable law.

---

## Schedule 1 — Security Measures (high-level)
- Identity and access management (RBAC)
- Logging and monitoring
- Secure SDLC practices
- Backup and recovery (where applicable)
- Vulnerability management
